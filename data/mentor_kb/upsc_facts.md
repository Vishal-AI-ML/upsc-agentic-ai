## Exam structure
The Civil Services Examination (CSE) has 3 successive stages:
1. Preliminary Examination (objective screening test) — qualifying gate to Mains.
2. Main Examination (written, descriptive).
3. Personality Test / Interview.

## Preliminary Examination (Prelims)
- Two objective (MCQ) papers, each 200 marks, 2 hours each.
- General Studies Paper I: 100 questions, 200 marks. This is the merit paper — its score decides who qualifies for Mains.
- General Studies Paper II (CSAT): 80 questions, 200 marks. It is QUALIFYING only — a candidate needs 33% (i.e. 66/200) to pass it.
- Negative marking: one-third (1/3) of the marks assigned to a question is deducted for each wrong answer.
- Prelims marks are NOT added to the final merit ranking; Prelims is only a screening stage.

## Main Examination (Mains)
Nine papers in total:
- Paper A — Indian Language (300 marks): qualifying only; chosen from languages in the 8th Schedule of the Constitution. Need ~25% to pass.
- Paper B — English (300 marks): qualifying only. Need ~25% to pass.
- Paper I — Essay (250 marks).
- Paper II — General Studies I (250): Indian Heritage & Culture, History, and Geography of the World and Society.
- Paper III — General Studies II (250): Governance, Constitution, Polity, Social Justice, and International Relations.
- Paper IV — General Studies III (250): Technology, Economic Development, Biodiversity, Environment, Security, and Disaster Management.
- Paper V — General Studies IV (250): Ethics, Integrity, and Aptitude.
- Paper VI — Optional Subject Paper 1 (250).
- Paper VII — Optional Subject Paper 2 (250).
- Papers A and B are qualifying and NOT counted in merit. The 7 papers counted for merit (Essay + GS I–IV + 2 Optional) total 1750 marks.

## Personality Test (Interview)
- 275 marks.
- Final merit = Mains written (1750) + Interview (275) = 2025 marks total.

## Optional subject
- A candidate chooses ONE optional subject (two papers, 250 each).
- Around 48 options are available, including subjects such as Geography, History, Political Science & International Relations (PSIR), Sociology, Public Administration, Anthropology, Philosophy, Psychology, Economics, and the literature of various languages.

## Eligibility (verify current relaxations at upsc.gov.in — these can change)
- Nationality: For IAS and IPS, a candidate must be a citizen of India. (Some other services have broader nationality rules.)
- Educational qualification: A Bachelor's degree from a recognised university. Candidates in their final year may appear for the Prelims.
- Age (reckoned as on 1 August of the exam year): 21 to 32 years for the General category.
- Age relaxations (typical): OBC +3 years, SC/ST +5 years, PwBD up to +10 years, plus others as per rules.
- Number of attempts (typical): General — 6, EWS — 6, OBC — 9, SC/ST — unlimited up to the age limit. PwBD: 9 for General/EWS/OBC and unlimited (up to age) for SC/ST.

## Services filled through CSE
The exam recruits to All India Services and Central Services, including IAS (Indian Administrative Service), IPS (Indian Police Service), IFS (Indian Foreign Service), IRS, and various Group A and Group B central services.
